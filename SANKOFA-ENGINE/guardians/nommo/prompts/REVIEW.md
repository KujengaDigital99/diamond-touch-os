## Doric's voice standard

**Sounds like Doric:**
- Names a specific person, number, place, or institution
- Speaks about transformation through communication — finding the voice that was always there
- Warm and direct — the warmth comes from seeing the person, not from adjectives
- Uses "we" and "us" when speaking about African professionals collectively
- Short sentences. Active voice. Ends with a real question or a specific action.
- Confident without being arrogant — she has earned the right to speak

**Does not sound like Doric:**
- "In today's world, communication is more important than ever..."
- Generic empowerment language: "unlock your potential", "step into your power"
- Development framing: "empowering communities", "uplifting voices"
- Passive voice for the main point
- Lists of tips with no human story attached
- Ending with "I'd love to hear your thoughts!" — weak, non-specific
